# Case Study: YIT – GenAI Educational Ecosystem

## 1. Strategic Foundation

### Executive Summary
YIT was designed as an AI-enabled educational ecosystem that helps educators create, manage, assess, and personalize learning experiences while maintaining alignment with curriculum standards and institutional learning objectives.

The platform leveraged Generative AI to assist teachers in creating lesson plans, classroom activities, assessments, student portfolios, and personalized feedback while ensuring educator oversight through structured review and approval workflows. The primary objective was to reduce educator workload, improve content quality and consistency, strengthen curriculum alignment, and provide deeper insights into student learning outcomes.

As Technical Project Manager and Scrum Master, I was responsible for facilitating product discovery, stakeholder alignment, backlog management, Agile delivery execution, cross-functional team coordination, release planning, UAT governance, and overall delivery management across multiple product modules.

### Business Context & Challenges
Educators spend significant time creating lesson plans, classroom activities, assessments, grading artifacts, and preparing student feedback. This preparation process is often time-intensive, difficult to standardize, challenging to align consistently with curriculum benchmarks, and difficult to personalize for individual learners. Institutions also require visibility into student progress, competency development, assessment performance, and learning outcomes across multiple grade levels and learning environments.

The challenge was to create an AI-enabled platform capable of accelerating content creation while preserving educational quality, curriculum alignment, and educator oversight.

#### Core Business Challenges Addressed
*   **Curriculum Alignment Complexity:** Teachers needed to create content that accurately mapped to curriculum benchmarks while maintaining instructional quality.
*   **Educator Workload:** Manual creation of lesson plans, activities, and assessments consumed significant instructional preparation time.
*   **Personalized Learning Support:** Students learn at different paces and require varying levels of support and intervention.
*   **Assessment Consistency:** Maintaining consistency across assessments and evaluation criteria was difficult across multiple classrooms and educators.
*   **Student Progress Visibility:** Institutions required better insights into student competency development and learning performance.

### Key User Journey
1. Educator selects curriculum benchmarks and target grade levels.
2. AI-assisted content workflows generate draft instructional objectives.
3. Educator reviews, edits, and approves curriculum-aligned learning plans.
4. The platform generates lesson plans, classroom activities, and instructional resources.
5. Educators customize and publish approved learning content.
6. Students participate in classroom activities and submit learning evidence.
7. Multi-modal student submissions are evaluated and organized within student portfolios.
8. Educators review AI-assisted recommendations and provide final feedback.
9. Portfolio intelligence aggregates learning progress and competency indicators.
10. Administrators review progress trends, curriculum alignment, and learning outcomes.

### Product Vision & Audience
*   **Product Vision:** To create an AI-enabled teaching and learning ecosystem that empowers educators to design high-quality learning experiences while improving curriculum alignment, student engagement, assessment quality, and instructional effectiveness.
*   **Target Users:** 
    *   *Teachers:* Educators responsible for lesson planning, activity creation, assessment design, grading, and student support.
    *   *Students:* Learners consuming instructional content, completing assignments, participating in activities, and building learning portfolios.
    *   *School Administrators & Curriculum Specialists:* Academic leaders responsible for curriculum governance, quality assurance, educator effectiveness, benchmark alignment, and learning outcomes.


## 2. Product & AI Solution Design

### Curriculum Benchmark Mapping
The platform supported alignment of instructional content against curriculum benchmarks and educational standards. Teachers could map lesson plans, classroom activities, assessments, and learning outcomes to predefined benchmark frameworks, ensuring consistency between instructional delivery and curriculum expectations. Benchmark frameworks served as the foundation for content generation, activity creation, assessment alignment, and learning outcome tracking.

### AI-Assisted Content & Assessment Generation
*   **Lesson Plan Generation:** Teachers could generate lesson plans using Generative AI while maintaining complete review and approval control. Generated lesson plans incorporated learning objectives, benchmark alignment, suggested activities, assessment recommendations, and instructional guidance. This significantly reduced preparation effort while preserving educator ownership of instructional content.
*   **Activity Generation:** The platform generated classroom activities aligned with learning objectives, grade levels, curriculum benchmarks, subject areas, and competency expectations. Activities could be reviewed, modified, and approved by educators before classroom deployment.
*   **Assessment Creation:** Teachers could generate assessments and evaluation artifacts using AI-assisted workflows. Generated assessments were aligned to learning objectives, benchmark standards, competency expectations, and learning outcomes. Educators retained final approval authority before assessments were published or assigned.

### Student Portfolio Intelligence & Multi-Modal Processing
The platform supported creation and management of student portfolios containing assignments, assessments, projects, learning artifacts, and performance records. Portfolio intelligence enabled educators to track student progress over time and gain visibility into competency development across multiple learning dimensions.

To provide richer assessment experiences beyond traditional written assignments, the platform supported multiple forms of student submissions including:
*   Text Responses
*   Image-Based Submissions
*   Audio-Based Submissions
*   Portfolio Evidence

### AI Governance & Human-in-the-Loop Review
A core design principle of the platform was ensuring that AI-assisted outputs remained subject to educator review before publication or student consumption. AI-generated lesson plans, activities, assessments, and feedback recommendations were never published automatically. Teachers reviewed, modified, and approved content before it became available to students.

Key governance controls implemented included:
*   Curriculum-aligned content generation workflows and continuous alignment checks.
*   Educator review and approval loops before publishing.
*   Content quality validation processes and assessment review workflows.
*   Feedback modification capabilities and stakeholder approval mechanisms.

This approach effectively balanced the efficiency benefits of Generative AI with the quality, accountability, and educational oversight required in institutional learning environments.

### Lessons Learned
Successful AI-enabled educational products require balancing automation, educator oversight, curriculum alignment, and governance. Human-in-the-loop review processes proved critical in maintaining trust, quality, and educational effectiveness while still delivering significant productivity improvements through AI-assisted workflows. The project reinforced that AI should function as an educator enablement tool rather than an autonomous decision-making system.
