
# Case Study: Cloud Data Modernization & AI Readiness Program (Project Raven)

## 1. Strategic Foundation

### Executive Summary

Project Raven focused on supporting enterprise data modernization initiatives by facilitating the migration of legacy on-premises data warehouse workloads to cloud-native analytics platforms.

The program enabled the conversion and modernization of workloads from legacy environments such as Teradata, Netezza, and Oracle to modern cloud data platforms including Snowflake, Google BigQuery, and AWS Redshift.

The objective was not only to modernize legacy data ecosystems but also to establish scalable, cloud-native data foundations capable of supporting advanced analytics, business intelligence, and downstream AI/ML initiatives.

As Technical Project Manager and Scrum Master, I supported delivery execution by coordinating migration activities, managing ticket workflows, facilitating communication between engineering and QA teams, supporting validation efforts, coordinating client UAT activities, and ensuring predictable delivery outcomes throughout the modernization lifecycle.

### Business Context & Challenges

Many organizations continue to operate critical reporting, analytics, and business intelligence workloads on legacy on-premises data warehouse platforms.

These environments often introduce challenges including:

* High maintenance costs
* Limited scalability
* Complex legacy SQL implementations
* Slow modernization initiatives
* Difficulty supporting advanced analytics and AI workloads
* Operational inefficiencies caused by fragmented data architectures

Organizations increasingly require cloud-native data ecosystems capable of supporting modern analytics, business intelligence, and AI-driven decision-making.

The challenge was to modernize large volumes of legacy workloads while maintaining business continuity, preserving data integrity, and minimizing disruption to downstream reporting processes.

#### Core Business Challenges Addressed

##### Legacy Platform Complexity

Organizations maintained extensive SQL workloads, stored procedures, and reporting logic developed over many years.

##### Query Conversion Challenges

Legacy SQL implementations frequently required translation and adaptation to function correctly within target cloud environments.

##### Validation Requirements

Converted workloads needed to be validated to ensure functional equivalence and business accuracy.

##### Quality Assurance Coordination

Migration activities generated significant validation tasks, defect reviews, and testing requirements across multiple teams.

##### Stakeholder Alignment

Successful delivery required coordination between engineering teams, QA teams, client stakeholders, and modernization program leadership.


### Product Vision

To accelerate enterprise data modernization by enabling efficient migration of legacy data workloads to cloud-native platforms while improving scalability, maintainability, analytics readiness, and support for future AI and machine learning initiatives.

### Key Stakeholders

#### Business & Client Stakeholders

Responsible for modernization objectives, reporting continuity, and business validation activities.

#### Engineering Teams

Responsible for workload conversion, migration execution, and technical implementation.

#### Quality Assurance Teams

Responsible for validation, defect identification, and migration quality assurance.

#### Program Leadership

Responsible for delivery governance, risk management, and modernization success.


### Key User Journey

1. Legacy workload and dependencies are identified for migration.
2. Migration and conversion requirements are analyzed.
3. SQL translation and workload conversion activities are performed.
4. Validation testing confirms expected functionality and data consistency.
5. Migration defects and conversion issues are identified and tracked.
6. Engineering teams implement required fixes and optimizations.
7. QA teams perform regression and validation testing.
8. Client stakeholders execute User Acceptance Testing (UAT).
9. Release readiness reviews are completed.
10. Modernized workloads are promoted to cloud-native environments and made available for downstream analytics and AI initiatives.

Large-scale modernization programs require strong coordination across engineering teams, QA teams, and business stakeholders.
